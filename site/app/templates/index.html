What is Mine for Change?

Mine for Change is project to raise funds in the form of cryptocurrency (Monero), for various non-profit and charity organizations.

Through the cummulative impact of volunteering users, great support and change can be made. As long as this site is open in a tab, or window of your device, you are contributing to your selected cause.

How do I use this?

Select an organization of your choice from the dropdown menu above. Click the start button.

By clicking the start button you agree to allow this site to use your computer for mining cryptocurrency

That's it! You are now helping to contribute to your chosen organization.

Be sure to keep this tab/window open to keep contributing.

You may chose a new organization at any time by selecting a new value, and pressing submit again.

How does this work?

When you select the organization of your choice, and click start, code running on the page is able to talk to your device, and tell it to a portion of its computing power to interact with the block chain. This is called "mining."

The block chain is a public ledger of past cryptocurrency transactions. Through "mining," your computer helps organize this ledger in such a way that ensures the fidelity and legitimacy of these transactions.

In exchange for helping with this process, your computer is awarded a new portion of a cryptocurrency. This portion is then sent to a receiving "addreses", which is the random sequence of numbers and letters which will display on the screen after pressing "Start."

For more detailed information head over to the Bitcoin wiki. While this site does not mine Bitcoin, the info here is general, and applicable for this case.

My computer is getting a bit warm, is this normal?

Yes. Mining is intentionally designed to be resource-intensive and difficult so that the number of blocks found each day by miners remains steady.

How can I trust this site?

Currently, this is a small project, with a singular address registered to the creators of the site. We pledge to donate any generated funds to the corresponding organizations - however also understand that there is no way for a user to verify this.

We are currently looking to get in touch with official representatives of such various organizations; if you are an employee of such a company, please send us an email at the contact below.

How can I help?

We're always trying to expand the number of organizations supported by this project. If you are a representative of a company and would like to have your cause be supported by this site, please reachout to us at mineforchange@protonmail.com.

Alternatively, submit a pull request via the repo to have your organization added.
